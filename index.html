02 — Brand Overview

Who We Are

New Found Visuals is a Chicago-based real estate photography and videography studio — a niche extension of LVN Studio built around a single belief: properties deserve to be seen as art. We exist at the intersection of architectural craft and cinematic storytelling.

Founded by Diego, NFV serves agents, developers, and luxury property marketers who understand that in a visual-first world, the quality of your media is the quality of your brand. We don't just shoot spaces — we build the case for why someone has to live there.

01

Studio DNA

Born from LVN Studio's creative lineage. Every shoot inherits a filmmaker's eye and a designer's precision.

02

Market Position

Premium tier. Higher-end listings, luxury developers, and agents who compete on quality — not price.

03

City Roots

Chicago is not a backdrop — it's a collaborator. The city's architecture, grid, and energy inform every frame.

04

The Offer

Photography, videography, drone, twilight — delivered as a cohesive editorial suite, not a commodity service.

Color Principles

Orange leads. It's the signal — used for emphasis, CTAs, and brand moments. Never dilute it with overuse. Amber supports warmth. Blue creates depth and contrast. Black is the canvas everything lives on.

Usage Ratio

Black: 70% · Orange: 15% · Amber: 8% · Blue: 5% · White: 2%. Color should feel intentional, not decorative. When in doubt, go darker.

07 — Logo System

The Mark

New Found Visuals

✓ Do

Use on dark backgrounds. Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "V" on all sides. Use orange for "New Found" only.

✓ Do

The graffiti word carries energy — let it be large. "VISUALS" in clean type provides the contrast. Both elements need each other.

✗ Don't

Never use on light backgrounds without inversion. Never distort, rotate, or recolor the lockup. Never separate the two typographic layers.

08 — Visual Direction

How We Shoot

Our visual language is cinematic, dark, and architectural. Every image is a decision — about light, angle, and story. We don't chase the safe shot. We build the definitive one.

Chicago's grid is our compositional DNA. Wide perspectives, deep sight lines, bold geometry. Interiors feel like movie sets. Exteriors feel like establishing shots. It's real estate as cinema.

01

Cinematic Light

Natural light as a starting point — golden hour, dramatic shadows. Every room should feel like a movie still.

02

Architectural Geometry

Lines, angles, symmetry. Frames should feel deliberately composed — nothing accidental, deep perspective always.

03

Dark Tonality

Even in bright spaces, the edit leans cinematic. Rich blacks, controlled highlights, color grading that references film.

04

Motion Matters

Slow drone pushes, gimbal walks, locked-off detail shots. Movement should feel intentional — never rushed.

05

Minimal Staging

Trust the space. Clean, editorial, aspirational. No clutter. The architecture is always the subject.

06

The Grid

Chicago's street grid is our visual metaphor — ordered, bold, infinite. Ordered composition. Bold framing.

10 — Applications

Brand In Use

01

Proposal Decks

Dark backgrounds. Syne headlines. One hero image per slide. Orange for callouts only. Client-facing decks feel like a lookbook, not a pitch.

02

Email Signatures

Name in Syne. Role in Space Grotesk uppercase. Contact in Inter. Orange for the NFV mark. No fluff — name, studio, link.

03

Delivery Galleries

Black background. Watermark-free. The work speaks — the brand doesn't need to scream in the gallery.

04

Video Titles & Outros

Minimal lower thirds. "New Found" in Permanent Marker, "VISUALS" in Syne. Orange on black. Hold 3 seconds. Out on a cut.

05

Merch & Physical

If it goes on a shirt, a box, a business card — black with white or orange type. The graffiti lockup is the hero graphic. No backgrounds, no gradients, no effects. Just the mark.

This is not videography. This is visual storytelling for places that earned it. And we're just getting started.
