02 — Brand Overview

Who We
Are

New Found Visuals is a Chicago-based real estate photography and videography studio — a niche extension of LVN Studio built around a single belief: properties deserve to be seen as art. We exist at the intersection of architectural craft and cinematic storytelling.

Founded by Diego, NFV serves agents, developers, and luxury property marketers who understand that in a visual-first world, the quality of your media is the quality of your brand. We don't just shoot spaces — we build the case for why someone has to live there.

Redefine real estate media with a cinematic, design-forward approach — making every property feel like an editorial feature, not a listing.

02

Photography, cinematic videography, floor plans, drone media, and social-ready content packages for luxury listings.

A streetwear-era creative sensibility applied to property media — bold, intentional, built for a social-first audience.

Usage Hierarchy

  • Void Black — all backgrounds and dark mode foundation
  • Cinematic Orange — logo, headlines, CTAs, active states
  • Electric Amber — hover states, warm accents, supporting moments
  • Lake Michigan Blue — contrast elements, cool accents, city/night context
  • Warm White — all copy and text; never use pure #FFFFFF
  • Carbon — card surfaces, panel elevation, section variation

Color Rules

  • Orange on black is the signature — protect it always
  • Orange + Blue together creates maximum cinematic impact
  • Amber is supporting only; never let it compete with Orange
  • The gradient (Orange → Amber → Blue) is reserved for progress and stripe accents
  • Always dark mode first across all brand touchpoints
  • Never use Cinematic Orange as background with white small text (contrast)

08 — Visual Direction

The
Aesthetic

Golden Hour Architecture
Warm ambient light, deep shadow detail, rich texture.
City at Blue Hour
Chicago skyline as backdrop.
Interior Cinematic
Controlled exposure, editorial depth.
01

Cinematic Framing

Wide-angle compositions emphasizing scale and drama. Every shot has a director's eye.

02

Light as Character

Natural light is the primary subject. Golden hour and blue hour preferred above all.

03

Tonal Restraint

Muted, moody color grading. Slightly desaturated mid-tones, never HDR. Never over-processed.

04

Urban Context

Chicago as a character in every shoot. Skylines and architecture provide context that elevates.

Photography Style

  • Expose for highlights — preserve window views and sky
  • Blend 3–5 natural light brackets for interiors, no flash-dominant
  • Muted, film-adjacent grade — warm shadows, cool mid-tones
  • Vertical and horizontal compositions for each key room
  • Detail shots: hardware, material texture, architectural elements
  • Consistent retouching: clean lawns, naturally enhanced skies

Video / Reel Style

  • Cinematic LUT inspired by film, not real estate video templates
  • Smooth gimbal with intentional start/end frames per sequence
  • Music-first editing — cuts timed to beat drops and transitions
  • Chicago establishing shots as intro or b-roll bookend
  • Drone used for context and drama, not just aerial overview
  • Text overlays in Syne Bold only — brand typeface exclusively
